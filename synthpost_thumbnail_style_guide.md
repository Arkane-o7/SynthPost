# SynthPost Thumbnail Style Guide

Purpose: define an original SynthPost thumbnail identity inspired by high-performing AI/news/explainer patterns, without copying any reference channel's branding, border, bug, or color system.

## 1. Brand Position

SynthPost thumbnails should feel:

- Premium, analytical, and global.
- Tech-forward, but not sci-fi generic.
- Serious, but still clickable.
- Fast to read on mobile.
- Editorial rather than spammy.
- Confident without overstating facts.

The house flavor: "Bloomberg terminal meets global AI briefing." Each thumbnail should look like a sharp visual argument, not a random collage.

## 2. Core Personality

SynthPost should not use a loud tabloid front-page identity. Instead, it should use a more premium visual system:

- Dark editorial base.
- Crisp type.
- One high-signal claim.
- One or two authoritative visual anchors.
- Data or map texture as proof context.
- Small, consistent SynthPost mark.
- Controlled accent color based on story emotion.

The thumbnail should imply: "This is a serious briefing, but the stakes are high."

## 3. Color Palette

### Core Colors

- Obsidian: `#070A0F`
- Graphite: `#111827`
- Slate: `#1F2937`
- Off-white: `#F8FAFC`
- Soft gray: `#CBD5E1`

### Accent Colors

- Signal cyan: `#18D4FF` for AI, data, chips, cloud, tools.
- Analysis blue: `#2563EB` for policy, global tech, enterprise.
- Alert red: `#EF233C` for bans, risk, crashes, warnings.
- Growth green: `#22C55E` for jobs, manufacturing, funding, rankings.
- Capital gold: `#F5C542` for money, deals, valuation, strategic importance.
- Sovereign saffron: `#FF8A00` for India policy or infrastructure, used sparingly.

### Color Rules

- Use one dominant accent per thumbnail, two maximum.
- Red is reserved for real conflict, warning, ban, collapse, or public backlash.
- Green is for growth, hiring, exports, market expansion, or national advantage.
- Cyan/blue is the default SynthPost AI-news treatment.
- Gold is for capital allocation, valuations, IPOs, and deal scale.
- Avoid all-yellow brand framing, heavy yellow borders, and black/yellow bug layouts.

## 4. Typography Direction

### Main Headline Font

Use a heavy, condensed sans with excellent mobile readability:

- Primary recommendation: Archivo Black or Archivo Expanded Black.
- Alternatives: Inter Tight Black, IBM Plex Sans Condensed Bold, Sora ExtraBold.

### Secondary Font

Use a modern neutral sans:

- Inter, Sora, IBM Plex Sans, or Space Grotesk.

### Type Rules

- Main thumbnail text: 3-5 words.
- Absolute max: 6 words.
- Main text size: 72-108 px at 1280x720.
- Secondary tag: 24-36 px, only if needed.
- Letter spacing: 0 or slightly positive. Never negative.
- Use all caps for main claims.
- Use Title Case only for person names, company names, or source labels.

## 5. Logo Usage

SynthPost logo treatment should be subtle and consistent:

- Place a small SP monogram or SynthPost wordmark in the top-left or bottom-left safe area.
- Size: 54-88 px wide for monogram, 120-170 px wide for wordmark.
- Use off-white/cyan on dark backgrounds or graphite on light panels.
- Do not use a large square yellow bug.
- Do not force the logo into every concept if it harms the composition; use metadata/frame consistency as the brand cue.

Recommended v1 mark location:

- Top-left corner with a slim cyan vertical bar.
- Keep it outside the main headline, not embedded in a copied "front page" badge.

## 6. Background Style

SynthPost backgrounds should look like premium news composites:

- Darkened real or generated editorial scenes.
- Data-center aisles, chip macro shots, factories, trading screens, documents, maps, satellite views, meeting rooms, protest photos, court/government buildings.
- Subtle grid, map, waveform, source-document, or terminal overlays.
- Soft depth blur behind text.
- Vignette to focus the frame.
- Controlled film grain to unify mixed assets.

Avoid:

- Generic blue AI fog.
- Random neon circuit backgrounds with no story meaning.
- Fake screenshots that look official.
- Decorative orbs, bokeh blobs, or purple gradient filler.

## 7. Subject Rules

### People

Use people when:

- The person is recognizable.
- The story is driven by a CEO, founder, minister, regulator, or public figure.
- Their expression communicates warning, confidence, conflict, or surprise.

Rules:

- One person: 40-60 percent of canvas height.
- Two people: make it a face-off only if there is real tension.
- Three people: only for summits, boards, market competition, or coalition stories.
- Unknown people need a strong company logo or object beside them.
- Use ethical cutouts. Do not manipulate expressions beyond normal color, crop, and contrast.

### Logos

Use logos as anchors, not wallpaper.

- Main logo: 18-32 percent of canvas width.
- Supporting logos: max two, smaller and clearly secondary.
- If three or more logos are needed, use a structured "market board" template.

### Maps and Flags

Use maps and flags when the story is geopolitical, regional, supply-chain, policy, or national-strategy driven.

- Use simplified silhouettes and route lines.
- Do not over-label.
- Avoid turning every India story into a flag collage.
- Prefer a premium dark map with one glowing route, marker, or conflict line.

### Devices, Chips, Servers, Factories

Use physical objects to make abstract AI stories tangible:

- Chips for model economics and hardware.
- Servers/data centers for compute and cloud.
- Factories for manufacturing and supply chains.
- Documents/stamps for bans, audits, lawsuits, and leaks.
- Phones/laptops for consumer product stories.

## 8. Headline Formula

SynthPost thumbnail text should be a compressed claim, not a miniature title.

Use one of these structures:

- `AUTHORITY + WARNING`
- `COUNTRY + RANKING`
- `COMPANY + BIG MOVE`
- `MODEL + UNDER PRESSURE`
- `DEAL SIZE + STRATEGIC BET`
- `OBJECT + CONSEQUENCE`
- `X VS Y`
- `THE HIDDEN COST`
- `INSIDE THE SHIFT`

Examples:

- `SATYA'S AI WARNING`
- `INDIA'S CHIP BET`
- `OPENAI UNDER PRESSURE`
- `$40B COMPUTE RACE`
- `CURSOR VS OPENAI`
- `THE SERVER SHORTAGE`
- `AI JOB SHOCK`
- `THE BAN BACKFIRED`

## 9. Layout System

### SynthPost Frame

Use one of three brand frames:

- No full border, only corner ticks.
- 3-5 px cyan/gold/red bottom rule based on story emotion.
- Thin graphite frame with one accented corner.

Avoid thick highlighter borders that mimic the reference channel.

### Common Layouts

1. Left portrait, right data object, top-left claim block.
2. Center object, two opposing faces, small bottom data chip.
3. Full-bleed map, one large face, headline in lower-left.
4. Factory/data center background, money number large, logo small.
5. Dark document board, red stamp, subject face cropped hard.

### Empty Space

- Leave 8-15 percent open dark space behind text.
- Empty space should be intentional, usually a dark panel or blurred background area.
- Do not fill every corner with logos.

## 10. Tone and Ethics

SynthPost can be dramatic but should not be misleading.

Use:

- "UNDER PRESSURE" instead of "DEAD" when the story is uncertain.
- "RISK SIGNAL" instead of "CRASH" when decline is early.
- "BAN SPARKS..." instead of "DREAM DIED" when the effect is debated.
- "INSIDE..." for analysis pieces where there is no breaking event.

Avoid:

- Fake quotes on thumbnail.
- Fabricated screenshots.
- Overstated death/collapse language.
- AI-generated likenesses of real people.
- Unverified logos or forged documents.

## 11. Mobile QA Checklist

At 160x90, a SynthPost thumbnail must pass:

- One subject is recognizable.
- Main text is readable.
- The emotional category is clear: warning, boom, conflict, mystery, or analysis.
- The main logo/object is not confused with another brand.
- Duration badge does not cover critical text.
- Nothing important is smaller than 18 px in the 320x180 downsample.

## 12. Premium Finish Checklist

- Unified color grade across all assets.
- Accurate cutout edges with subtle rim light.
- No low-resolution faces.
- No stretched logos.
- One dominant focal point.
- Text has a clean shadow or solid panel.
- Background blur/noise is deliberate.
- Export is crisp at 1280x720 PNG and under 2 MB if possible.
